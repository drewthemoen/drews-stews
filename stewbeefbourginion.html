    Stew Analysis



    If you are in the position I was in once, you may be surprised to find out that beef bourguignon is indeed a stew, and it does not mess around. This is one of the more difficult stews to prepare, with several recipes topping out at over 3 hours, but if you can handle the slog, you're in for one of the fullest, most flavorful stews out there.


    Beef Bourguignon, also known as Beef Burgundy, is a French stew that comes from the Burgundy region of France. It's called that because it's cooked with red wine. Since the meat can be tough, this is why it takes so long to cook this way. This cooking style is known as braising. First searing, then cooking at a low temperature for a very long time.



    Fundamental Ingredients

    ★ In spite of the cost compared to some other recipes I've showed you so far, beef bourguignon was actually originally a peasant dish. Slow-cooking meant tough cuts of meat became palatable.



    Below, I've included a checklist for the most necessary ingredients.

  • Beef.50% beef
  • Red wine base.50% bourguignon
  • Thickening Agent, like flour.
  • Onions, carrots, garlic.
  • Mushrooms.
  • Spices: thyme, bay leaf, salt, pepper.

    • ★ There are other types of bourguignons as well. One such type is called Mushroom Bourguignon
