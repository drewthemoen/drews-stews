    Stew Analysis



    Goulash is a type of Hungarian stew made with meat, vegetables, paprika, and other spices. At the time it was created, which was possibly a thousand years ago, it was a simple dish which could be easily be prepared by adding sun-dried ingredients with water. That meant you could keep it for a long time, and whip it up whenever you got hungry. It's come a long way since then, and is arguably one of the most well-known stews in the world to this day!


    Goulash can be eaten on its own, or paired with bread and other sides. Much like other stews, it plays very well with others. With this stew in particular, however, it is important to use a large pot. This helps prevent burning, overflow, and makes sure that there is enough to give a bowl to Drew.



    Fundamental Ingredients

    ★ As you can see, Goulash will look very different depending on what you put in it!
    Today, there are even varieties of vegan Goulash that use lentils and mushrooms instead of meat.



    Below, I've included a checklist for the most necessary ingredients. Feel free to use it for your own Goulash creations! bad pun, sorry.

  • Paprika! You can add other spices, too.
  • Any type of meats or lentils and mushrooms.
  • Any kind of fat like lard or oil.
  • Onions. These will add layers to the stew.

    • ★ You can make a variety of different types of Goulash by switching up just a few ingredients. For example, using mutton is part of a traditional Hungarian style called Mutton Gulyás.
