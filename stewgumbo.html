    Stew Analysis



    The origins of gumbo are a bit unclear, but it is believed to have been influenced by combining African, French, and Native American cuisines. The name gumbo is thought to have originated from the West African word ki ngombo, which refers to okra, a key ingredient in many gumbo recipes.

    Another ingredient that sets some types of gumbo apart is filé powder, a spice made from ground sassafras that gives gumbo a savory flavor. It also thickens up the stew a little, kind of like okra.

    One last thickener which sets Gumbo apart is the usage of roux as a thickening agent. A roux is made by cooking flour and fat together. This may have been influenced by French peasants who used roux to thicken their stews. Gumbo is so rich with influence from different cultures that it stands out as a stew.

    Fundamental Ingredients

    ★ Gumbo, having come from Louisiana, is of course the official cuisine of Louisiana.



    Below, I've included a checklist for some of the more popular ingredients.

  • Roux. Flour and fat.
  • Any type of meats or vegan alternatives.
  • Celery, very crunchy,
  • Bell peppers, also crunchy
  • Onions. These will add layers to the stew.
  • Okra or filé powder for thickening.
  • Spices. Thyme, bay leaf, cayenne, S&P.
  • Rice for heartiness.
