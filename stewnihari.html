    Stew Analysis



    Nihari is a very flavorful slow-cooked stew that originates from the Indian subcontinent. Usually, you make it with beef or lamb, but like with pretty much every other entry on this website, you can use different types of ingredients to suit your tastes.

    The name Nihari comes from the Arabic word Nahar, meaning "day" This is because the stew was consumed in the morning following Fajr prayer.`

    A large part of what makes Nihari so good and unique is in the Nihari masala powder used to season it. It is a potent blend of fennel, ginger, cardamom, onion, and much more. This is another stew where time is an important ingredient.
