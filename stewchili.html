    Stew Analysis



    When first creating this project, I swore I wasn't going to show any blatant favoritism. Once I entered the caption for this page, however, I realized that I had made my choice, and that there was no way I that could possibly go back and change it. Chili is, without a doubt, my favorite stew. It is my father's best recipe.

    Chili, or chili con carne (with meat), is a spicy stew that came from the American Southwest. It's made with chili peppers, and that is where its heat and iconic name come from.

    There's a plethora of different types of chili, from Texas chili (which has no beans) to Cincinnati chili (which is served over spaghetti).



    Fundamental Ingredients

    ★ Despite bringing the heat, chili peppers actually help battle inflammation and are full of other vitamins. Learn more here.



    Below, I've included a checklist for the most necessary ingredients. I hope you will use even more.

  • Chili peppers, naturally. Can be fresh or dried.
  • Protein, beef most commonly.
  • Onions and garlic
  • Kidney beans, pinto beans, black beans. Any beans!
  • Spices. Oregano, cumin, S&P.
  • Other veggies, like celery.

    • ★ Chili is so popular and so varied in the ways it can be made that there are heated cook-offs to see who can make the best specific chili.
