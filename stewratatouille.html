    Stew Analysis



    My first introduction to this stew was through the Pixar movie of that same name. I'm leading with this because that movie had a big impact on me as a kid, and this was also referenced in almost every online recipe I found for ratatouille. For what it's worth, all forms of ratatouille I've had are incredible. Ratatouille is a traditional French stewed vegetable dish. Typically, it's made of sliced vegetables like eggplant, tomatoes, and others.



    "Just cut off the burnt parts! It's probably fine."


    There's a another peculiar thing about this stew. That is because, and it pains me to admit this, that it isn't always a stew. That's right. Ratatouille is sort of like Schrodinger's Stew depending on how you make it. I made sure to include a photo of its stewed variety, but you will soon see another variant known as Confit Byaldi. In that case, you finish off by baking it.
    This is actually the variant shown in the movie!
