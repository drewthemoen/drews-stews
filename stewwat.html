    Stew Analysis



    Wat is an Ethiopian and Eritrean stew. If you’ve seen this stew around before, you might be familiar with it in the form of Doro Wat, which specifically incorporates chicken. But again, like pretty much all other stews in this bracket, switching up even a couple of ingredients is gonna get you an entirely different stew. The one link they are all for the most part going to have in common is the use of berbere spice. This is what gives Wat its kick.


    Another component synonmyous with Wat is niter kibbeh, a type of clarified butter. It's like ghee, another type of clarified butter featured on the Nihari page. Those types of butters work great if you still want the buttery flavor without risking the butter burning right away.



    Fundamental Ingredients
