    THE FINALE

    Hello! My name is Drew, and this is a website for all of my favorite stews!

    First off, welcome to my website, and thank you for taking the time to look around!

    This is a little project I've had on my mind for the better part of the semester, and I'm excited to finally show it off to the world! (or at least anyone who comes across it!) May this website serve as an icon for my love of cooking and stews in particular. I hope you'll find a lot to do here! I included a stew-themed quiz, contact pages, and of course a few pages on stews!

    Up near the top of every page, you'll also find a handy dandy little navigation menu, a way to bookmark any page, mess around with the look of the page, and a button to a random stew page if you get bored!

    Now, the first question you may be asking at this very moment is: what is a stew? I'm glad you asked! A stew, at its core, is any kind of dish that uses ingredients cooked in a liquid base, like stock or broth, for example. Stews are cooked slowly over a long time with heat for everything to get to know each other in the pot.

    "But isn't that just soup?" you may ask. NO! Well, maybe. Sometimes?
    To be perfectly honest, the line between soup and stew can be as foggy as clam chowder. The consensus I seemed to find online and from various recipe pages, is that stews tend to be thicker than soups, using less liquid and more solid foods.

    Final Thoughts


    Some of the best things in life can come with time.




    Did you know there's a type of stew that can potentially last for years without ever going bad? This is known as perpetual stew. Basically, you keep adding fresh ingredients and liquid to the stew while simmering, and as long as it is properly cared for, it can last for decades.

    I was skeptical at first. After all, perishable foods have to go bad after a while, right? Well, they do, but that's where the constant care by addition of fresh foods and a nice inferno come in. I think that's salient advice that goes even beyond stew: with watchful care and proper maintenance, something may last longer than you'd think.
